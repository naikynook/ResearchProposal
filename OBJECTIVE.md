# Overall Objective

## Project
**Smart Room** is a research proposal for an interactive room that leverages machine learning, aesthetics, and vision technology to create an immersive experience with the user while collecting data in a malicious way.

## Core Intent
The project does not primarily propose policy for vision technology. Instead, it raises awareness of practices already underway—government regulation gaps, front-end/back-end disconnects, and what biometric data extraction will mean for advertising, policing, and everyday life—so that people can recognize how surveillance and extraction operate behind polished interfaces.

## Capstone Gesture
An interactive room with projection screens and a camera responds to the user through visuals and audio. The experience is immersive and beautiful enough that the participant is not thinking about data collection—until the interaction reveals what was happening behind the scenes.

## Dual Structure
1. **Visuals** — immersive, aesthetic, intentionally non-threatening surfaces that draw the user in.
2. **Vision technology** — camera input driving screen output, revealing extraction, demographics of training data, and the ethics of biometric systems.

## Research Anchors
- Awareness of facial recognition in policing and predictive surveillance (Flock, Palantir, Snowden’s lineage).
- Techno-activism as trajectory rather than pure policy proposal.
- Digital art, concert visuals, and interactive spaces as bridges between vision tech and the user.
- Community of practice: Adam Harvey (Exposing AI, VFRAME, CV Dazzle), Lauren McCarthy (LAUREN), Forensic Architecture.
- Situated theory: Zuboff and Srnicek—tracking and data extraction as forces widening the wealth gap.

## Methods & Form
Web design, projections, coding, data visualization, and graphic art; work with public biometric datasets (e.g. MegaFace); outputs likely include an exhibition, a website, and static data visualizations. Tooling leans on open-source stacks (P5, D3, paper.js, OpenCV) rather than building vision systems from scratch.

## This Website
This site is primarily a **repository of thoughts** for the upcoming proposal: a living archive of interim research text, experiments, and visual references as the proposal develops.
